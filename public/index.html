What changed

On 1 May the playing field changed.

The Renters' Rights Act 2025 is the biggest overhaul of UK private tenancy law in 30 years. But your agent is still sending out contracts from a template written before any of this happened.

Before — the old rules
  • "No-fault" Section 21 evictions — no reason needed
  • Locked-in 6 & 12-month fixed terms
  • Landlords could refuse pets outright
  • Rent rises with little structure or notice
Now — the Renters' Rights Act 2025
  • Section 21 abolished — evictions need a valid legal reason
  • All new tenancies are periodic (rolling) — fixed terms gone
  • Pet requests can't be unreasonably refused
  • Rent rises once a year, with two months' written notice

Once you sign, the burden of fighting an illegal clause is on you. A 60-second check beats six months of regret.
